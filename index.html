About Me

Supporting NOAA as a Junior Scientist, I specialize in the processing and verification of Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) acquisitions over active hurricanes. I bring a strong technical focus to the role by writing Python scripts to automate analytical processing chains, while also leveraging ArcGIS Pro to translate raw data into professional-grade spatial visualizations.

In May 2019, I graduated from Emory University with a B.S. in Environmental Sciences and a concentration in Earth and Atmospheric Sciences. During my time there, I worked as a researcher and intern on geospatial projects for the Emory Center for Digital Scholarship's Georgia Coast Atlas, utilizing GIS software and aerial imagery to measure shoreline erosion on Georgia’s barrier islands.

I went on to earn a Master of Professional Science in Applied Remote Sensing from the University of Miami in 2020. While completing my degree, I served as a Graduate Teaching Assistant for Marine GIS and conducted research on SAR imagery. This research was mentored by my current team at NOAA and serves as the foundation for my current work. Since joining NOAA, I have focused on strengthening my skills in programming, geospatial analysis, and active remote sensing to support operational mission requirements.

In addition to my professional focus on Earth and atmospheric sciences, I am deeply passionate about planetary science. In my spare time, I self-teach the exploration and analysis of HiRISE Mars data to expand my remote sensing expertise to other planetary bodies. As a hobby, I also enjoy reading extensively on the history and future of space exploration.
